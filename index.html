Ahmad Shah Baba


Ahmad Shah Baba, also known as Ahmad Shah Durrani, founded the Durrani empire in 1774, laying the foundation for modern Afghanistan. Revered as the "Father of the Nation," he united the Afghan tribes, expanded his empire across South and Central Asia, and established a legacy of Afghan sovereignty and resilience. His leadership and military prowess earned him a lasting place in the history of the region.

Here's a timeline of Ahmad Shah Durrani (Baba)'s life:
  • 1722 - Born in Herat
  • 1747 - He had established himself as a formidable leader, uniting various Afghan tribes under his rule. This year marked the founding of the Durrani empire, with Ahmad Shah proclaimed as its first ruler.
  • 1757 - He led a successful expedition to conquer Delhi, establishing control over significant parts of present-day India. His strategic prowess and diplomatic skills allowed him to forge alliances and extend his influence across South and Central Asia.
  • 1777 - After a reign marked by significant achievements and challenges, Ahmad Shah Baba passed away, leaving behind a legacy as the "Father of the Nation" in Afghanistan. His leadership and vision for a united Afghan state continue to inspire reverence and admiration, shaping the course of Afghan history for generations to come.
